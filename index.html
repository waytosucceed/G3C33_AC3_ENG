    Simple Present Tense
    The simple present tense is used to describe habits, general truths, and regular actions. The simple present tense is like talking about your daily routine.

    Rule: Subject + Verb in the base form/third person plural form + the rest of the sentence

    For He/She/It (add 's' or 'es' to the verb):
    He runs fast.

    For I/You/We/They (use the base form of the verb):
    I like to read.
    Present Continuous Tense
    We use this tense to talk about actions happening right now or around this time.

    Rule: Subject + am/is/are + present participle (verb+ing) + the rest of the sentence

    We use:
    • "is" for he/she/it
    • "am" for I
    • "are" for you/we/they
    Using "is" for he/she/it:
    He is running fast.

    Using "am" for I:
    I am reading a book.

    Using "are" for you/we/they:
    You are playing with the ball.
